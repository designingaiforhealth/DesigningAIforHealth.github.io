About the Workshop

Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) solutions continue to promise a transformational impact on the ways in which health is sought and delivered. However, the translation of such solutions from the lab to the real world has been slow and challenging. A key aspect of this translation is the design of human-AI interactions, which presents both significant opportunities and challenges.

This workshop aims to bring together a multidisciplinary group of designers, researchers, and practitioners from HCI, AI, healthcare, and other relevant fields to reinvigorate how AI solutions are conceptualized and designed in the context of health and to articulate a future agenda for research, design, and evaluation of such systems.

Workshop Objectives

  • ✓Identify practices and challenges of designing for different stakeholders.
  • ✓Discuss methods for conceptualizing, designing, and evaluating human-AI interaction.
  • ✓Examine current guidelines, note gaps, and articulate new design principles.
  • ✓Articulate a future agenda for the research community.
  • ✓Bring together a multidisciplinary group of designers, practitioners, and researchers.

Topics & Research Questions

We invite discussion and submissions on a range of topics critical to the future of AI in health.

Applying Design Guidelines

How do we apply existing design guidelines in the unique, risk-averse context of healthcare?

Automation & Human Control

How can we operationalize human-centeredness by balancing automation and user control?

AI in User Workflows

What does true workflow integration mean for both clinicians and consumers, and how is it achieved?

Adapting Design Methods

What are the practices, challenges, and strategies to "design the right thing" and "design the thing right"?

Evaluation & Measurement

What should be evaluated in human-AI systems for health, and how do we measure what truly matters?

Beyond Traditional Themes

Exploring work that goes beyond explainable AI and trust to forge new paradigms in interaction design.

Workshop Schedule

Our workshop will take place over two 90-minute sessions, designed for active participation and collaboration.

Icebreaker & Introductions

30 minutes

Participants and organizers will introduce themselves and share learnings on a collaborative board.

Lightning Talks (3 Sessions)

90 minutes (3 x 30 mins)

Authors of accepted papers will present their work in thematically organized sessions.

Table Discussions & Brainstorming

45 minutes

Participants will break into groups to discuss key questions and topics emerging from the talks.

Group Reflection & Report Out

15 minutes

Organizers summarize insights, engage in a broader discussion, and outline post-workshop plans.
